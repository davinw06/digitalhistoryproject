Belize as an Export Hub: British Exploitation of Labour and Resources and the Neglect of Infrastructure

By common knowledge, most are aware that the British came to the Americas for exploration, and eventually ended up exploiting the land for its resources, bringing slaves with them to accomplish this task. Focusing on Belize, which housed British colonizers exporting mahogany wood back to Britain after slaves shopped them down. While they were here, they set up only small camps, not meant to last, to work from. This indicates that the British did not intend to actually build on this land or improve its economy in any way. Due to this, it was left at the hands of the Belizeans that migrated here at the time from neighbouring lands, and the previously enslaved Afri-Belizeans to build the infrastructure and the economy of Belize Honduras.
